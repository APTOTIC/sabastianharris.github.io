I intervened but I was too far away to be of any Immedient use. The incident made me worry about my wife's safety, she didn’t own a phone, and her mother kept her sheltered and refused to buy her one, Her health conditions prevented her from working, so she had no ability to provide for herself, and my wife mother was her payee, so any money that went to my wife, stayed in her mothers pocket, on top of making money for taking care of her own daughter through IHSS, So I bought her a phone, I wasn’t making much, so I bought a cheap phone from seven eleven and got the only plan I could afford, then during a phone call I heard another altercation, and I heard a loud bang, I was very worried and stayed on the phone, I got my keys and started walking to my car to drive down, and before I took off my wife picked up the phone and told me not to come, and she told me her mother smashed her phone, I saw the phone the next day, not much damage was done, but the back battery compartment wouldn’t stay on, so I bought her another phone with a case and asked her to find another living situation. We ended up getting married, and she moved in with me and my parents, she ended up being homesick, regardless of how her mother treated her, she missed her mom, so she ended up moving back, we were married, but not under the same roof, we would call each other often and I heard yet again another altercation.

In 2015 – Due to all the altercations, I ended up having a plan that I thought would solve all the problems. In 2016 - I ended up truly homeless because of all the legal trouble. My wife and I had a shared opinion, no one person, or any form of the government, or the justice system, has a right to say weather me and my wife can be together, only we have the right to make that discission, and my wife didn’t want me to give up, We made the neutral discission to disobey, that opened my life to being the type of felon that the justice system loves to go after. Every time I was released I refused to report to probation or report to BIP, I was homeless, and I had to beg for small change just to eat at my local fast food restaurant, so I refused to pay any money, and I kept getting arrested for misdemeanor restraining order violation, and felony probation violation. Each time I got arrested I would spend longer in jail then the last, then a judge threatened me with 5 years in prison if I violated again.

In 2017 – After I was released, I still didn’t listen, we ran to los angeles, about 6 hours south from where I lived, I was still homeless, and still begging for money. While we were in Los Angeles, we finally had our first real Halloween and thanksgiving since we got married, but then in December, my wife and I slept on the sidewalk outside a church on Gower street in Hollywood until morning, where they served hot breakfast to the homeless, when she woke up she was spitting up blood, and she couldn’t breathe, I dialed 911, and the ambulance came, and they took her to saint josephs hospital in Burbank where she was diagnose with pneumonia, and she died 3 days later.
